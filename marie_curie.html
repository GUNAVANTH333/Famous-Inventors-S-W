Radioactivity

Marie Curie conducted groundbreaking research on radioactivity, a term she coined. Her work led to the discovery of the elements radium and polonium.

About Marie Curie

Marie Curie was a physicist and chemist who pioneered research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and remains the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields.

Details About Her Discoveries

Radioactivity: Marie Curie’s discoveries revolutionized the field of science, particularly in medical applications like X-ray imaging.
