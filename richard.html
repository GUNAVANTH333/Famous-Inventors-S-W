Steam Locomotive

Trevithick built the first steam locomotive in 1804, capable of hauling loads over iron rails, marking the beginning of railway transportation.

About Richard Trevithick

Richard Trevithick was a British engineer and inventor who played a key role in the development of steam power. He is remembered as one of the pioneers of locomotive technology.

Details About His Work

High-Pressure Steam Engine: Trevithick improved steam engines by using higher pressure, making them smaller, more powerful, and efficient.

Steam Locomotive: His innovations led to the creation of the first functional railway steam locomotive, an invention that revolutionized transport and industry.
