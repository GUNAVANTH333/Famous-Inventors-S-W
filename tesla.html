About Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla (1856-1943) was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist. He is best known for his revolutionary work in alternating current (AC) systems, which transformed global power distribution.

Details About His Inventions

Alternating Current (AC) System: Tesla's AC system made long-distance electricity transmission possible, becoming the foundation of modern power grids.

Tesla Coil: A high-voltage transformer that played a crucial role in wireless communication and radio technology.

Wireless Energy Transfer: Tesla envisioned a world where energy could be transmitted without wires. His experiments laid the groundwork for technologies like wireless charging.
